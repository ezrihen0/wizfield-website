# WIZFIELD Marketing Website — Master Plan v1

**Document status:** Working master plan for the official WIZFIELD marketing website  
**Project scope:** Separate from the WIZFIELD SaaS application repo. This plan governs the marketing website strategy, information architecture, copy direction, visual direction, page architecture, and implementation sequencing before code begins.  
**Primary outcome:** Build a premium SaaS website that converts qualified field-service owners into trial starts while staying fully aligned with WIZFIELD product truth.

---

# 0. Executive Summary

The official WIZFIELD marketing website must present WIZFIELD as:

> **The intelligent operating system for field-service businesses.**

It must not present WIZFIELD as:
- a generic CRM,
- a narrow chimney-only tool,
- a fake “autonomous AI runs everything” product,
- or a template-style SaaS landing page with disconnected visuals and soft claims.

The website must sell four ideas together:

1. **Operational Control**  
   Calls, customers, jobs, estimates, invoices, messaging, scheduling, reports, and customer access belong in one coordinated operating system.

2. **Intelligent Assistance**  
   WIZFIELD surfaces operational intelligence, supports follow-up workflows, and includes carefully bounded AI capabilities without pretending to replace human operators.

3. **Growth Enablement**  
   Growth Center turns operational signals into marketing opportunities, drafts, campaigns, publishing workflows, and internal analytics — with explicit human control over publishing.

4. **Scalable Business Structure**  
   WIZFIELD supports owners running one or more service businesses through multi-business workspace control, shared billing architecture, and organization-scoped operating context.

The visual identity must remain locked to the canonical WIZFIELD Brand DNA:

> **Bright operational clarity + glass intelligence surfaces + cyan-to-violet digital energy + premium spacing discipline.**

# 3. Website Mission

## 3.1 What the website must accomplish

The website must:

1. Explain what WIZFIELD is in seconds.
2. Create desire through a premium, future-facing visual system.
3. Establish product credibility through real, specific, grounded capability.
4. Convert traffic into **trial starts**.
5. Route larger accounts toward **Business / Contact Sales**.
6. Support future search and content authority.
7. Stay legally and commercially safe by avoiding unsupported claims.

## 3.2 What the website must avoid

The website must avoid:

- vague SaaS filler copy,
- fake testimonials,
- inflated AI promises,
- diagrams that look beautiful but explain nothing,
- dozens of weak feature blurbs with no product thesis,
- design drift away from the WIZFIELD brand system,
- converting “smart” into “autonomous” when product truth does not support it.

# 6. Audience Definition

## 6.1 Primary audience

The primary buyer is:

> **An owner-operator or growing field-service business owner who has outgrown fragmented tools and wants operational clarity, professional customer workflows, and room to scale.**

## 6.2 Primary audience profile

- Owns or manages a field-service company.
- Runs real jobs and customer operations daily.
- Feels the pain of missed follow-ups, fragmented records, scattered communication, and messy team processes.
- Is willing to adopt software if it clearly reduces chaos and supports growth.
- Does not want an enterprise monster.
- Does not want a toy app.
- Responds well to visual professionalism, operational credibility, and concrete examples.

## 6.3 Industry framing

The website should lead with broad field-service relevance but may reinforce credibility with language such as:

> **Built from real field operations. Designed for the next generation of service businesses.**

Supported or target categories may include:
- Chimney & Fireplace
- HVAC
- Plumbing
- Electrical
- Roofing & Gutter
- Garage Doors
- Pest Control
- Restoration
- Appliance Repair
- Related service trades

The site must not falsely imply that every vertical already has fully specialized, bespoke workflows unless that becomes true product scope later.

# 12. Visual Design System Translation for the Website

## 12.1 Locked visual thesis

> **Bright Operational Clarity  
> + Glass Intelligence Surfaces  
> + Cyan-to-Violet Digital Energy  
> + Precision Typography  
> + Controlled Premium Spacing  
> = WIZFIELD**

## 12.2 Surface usage

| Surface | Visual intensity |
|---|---|
| Hero | Highest expressive intensity |
| Product feature sections | Medium intensity |
| Pricing | Clean and premium |
| Docs strip | Calm authority |
| Footer | Refined, simple, stable |

## 12.3 Color roles

| Color | Role |
|---|---|
| Liquid Platinum | Main canvas |
| Digital Indigo | authority / text emphasis |
| Electric Cyan | visible intelligence / interaction energy |
| Hyper Violet | depth / AI and premium-tech balance |
| Plasma Pink | rare alert / micro attention only |

## 12.4 Typography

- Display / logo character: Ethocen direction where appropriate.
- Interface and body copy: Inter.
- Avoid decorative typography outside brand moments.

## 12.5 Glass rules

Use glass for:
- hero panels,
- flagship UI mockups,
- AI highlight cards,
- select system visual elements.

Do not use glass:
- on every content card,
- in dense copy blocks where legibility suffers,
- as a crutch instead of real hierarchy.

## 12.6 Motion principles

Approved:
- subtle ambient glow breathing,
- gentle line energy,
- slow node pulse,
- premium card lift,
- CTA shimmer restraint.

Rejected:
- bouncing,
- neon flicker,
- hyperactive transitions,
- constant distracting motion.

# 13. Product Truth and Forbidden Claims Register

## 13.1 AI forbidden claims

Do **not** write:
- “AI runs your business automatically.”
- “AI dispatches technicians autonomously.”
- “AI sends SMS/email without you.”
- “Self-learning AI that gets smarter every day automatically.”
- “Full AI phone replacement for your team 24/7.”
- “Guaranteed ROI.”
- “Guaranteed lead volume.”
- “AI books jobs into the calendar with firm commitments.”
- “AI updates pricebooks automatically.”

## 13.2 Growth Center forbidden claims

Do **not** write:
- “Auto-publishes your content.”
- “Posts automatically to Meta and Google.”
- “Complete social media autopilot.”
- “Growth Center shows ROI or ad dashboard performance” if that is not actual implemented product truth.
- “Instagram publishing” as shipped V1 if outbound publishing remains deferred.

## 13.3 Language Store forbidden claims

Do **not** write:
- “Translate the whole product instantly.”
- “Customer documents are fully bilingual.”
- “All SMS and emails are translated automatically.”
- “All legal/compliance content is translated.”
- “Every customer-facing field is supported.”

## 13.4 Multi-Business forbidden claims

Do **not** write:
- “Cross-business analytics across all businesses at once.”
- “Unified cross-org intelligence dashboard.”
- Any statement implying tenant boundaries are blended.
