## 2. WIZFIELD Brand Voice and Copy Principles

### Tone of Voice

WIZFIELD should sound:

- Direct.
- Confident.
- Intelligent.
- Grounded.
- Practical.
- Premium.
- Operator-aware.
- Future-facing without hype.

WIZFIELD should not sound:

- Cute.
- Casual to the point of weakness.
- Overly corporate.
- Robotic.
- Breathless.
- Buzzword-heavy.
- Fake-enterprise.
- Generic field-service CRM.

### Sentence Length Preference

Use mostly short to medium sentences.

Good pattern:

- Headlines should be direct and memorable.
- Subheadlines can be one strong sentence or two concise sentences.
- Supporting paragraphs should usually stay under 35 words.
- Bullets should be concrete, not slogan-like.

Avoid long multi-clause claims that try to sell everything at once.

### Headline Style

Headlines should:

- Name the operating problem or product thesis clearly.
- Use strong nouns: operations, system, context, workflow, control, intelligence, growth, workspace.
- Prefer clarity over cleverness.
- Avoid generic headline formulas like `Work smarter, not harder` unless grounded by concrete product proof.

Preferred headline patterns:

- `Run your field-service business from one intelligent operating system.`
- `Every field-service workflow connected in one place.`
- `AI that helps operators see, decide, and follow through.`
- `Turn operational signals into marketing momentum.`
- `Run one business — or several — without losing context.`

### Subheadline Style

Subheadlines should explain what the headline means in product terms.

Use:

- Specific modules.
- Concrete workflow language.
- Bounded intelligence language.
- Buyer-relevant outcomes such as clarity, control, follow-through, structure.

Avoid:

- Vague claims like `everything you need to grow`.
- Unsupported outcomes like `increase revenue instantly`.
- Empty adjectives like `powerful`, `seamless`, or `revolutionary` unless paired with specific proof.

### CTA Style

CTA labels should be short, action-oriented, and consistent.

Primary CTA is always:

> Start Trial

Use secondary CTAs only for lower-friction education or high-intent routing:

- See How It Works
- Explore Platform
- Explore WIZFIELD AI
- Explore Growth Center
- Explore Language Store
- Explore Multi-Business
- View Pricing
- View Full Pricing
- Contact Sales
- Explore Docs

Do not introduce alternate CTA labels casually.

### Technical vs Emotional Balance

The copy should be about 70% practical and 30% emotional.

Practical copy explains:

- Calls.
- Leads.
- Jobs.
- Estimates.
- Invoices.
- Communication.
- Reports.
- Customer access.
- AI drafts and signals.
- Growth workflows.
- Workspace switching.

Emotional copy should express:

- Operational clarity.
- Confidence.
- Control.
- Less fragmentation.
- Professionalism.
- Readiness to scale.

Avoid over-indexing on emotion without product proof.

### Operating-System Thesis Language

Use `operating system` to mean:

- Connected field-service workflows.
- One coordinated operating picture.
- Shared context across modules.
- Business records and customer workflows moving together.
- Workspace control for one or more businesses.

Do not use `operating system` to imply:

- Autonomous business operation.
- AI replacing staff.
- AI dispatching technicians.
- Full automation of field-service operations.

### Real Field Credibility

Use real field credibility as support, not the main brand category.

Safe framing:

> Built from real field operations. Designed for modern service businesses.

Avoid shrinking WIZFIELD into one trade. Do not position the brand as chimney-only.

### Premium Without Vagueness

Premium copy should feel clean because it is precise.

Use:

- Operational clarity.
- Premium system.
- Product discipline.
- Intelligent assistance.
- Controlled workflows.
- Separated context.

Avoid:

- Luxury language with no product meaning.
- Overused SaaS claims like `unlock potential`, `supercharge your team`, `next-level productivity`.
- Abstract claims unsupported by UI or product truth.

Primary problem message:

Supporting problem lines:

- Your business is not disorganized. Your tools are.
- Calls and customer context get separated.
- Jobs, estimates, and invoices stop telling one story.
- Follow-ups depend on memory instead of a system.
- Marketing gets detached from what is actually happening in the field.
- Team complexity grows faster than the tools supporting it.

### C. Solution-Level Message

Primary solution message:

> WIZFIELD turns fragmented field-service workflows into one connected operating system.

Supporting solution lines:

- Keep the operating picture visible from first call to paid invoice.
- Carry context forward instead of rebuilding the story at every step.
- Connect operations, intelligence, growth workflows, and workspace control.
- Give owners a system built for real work now and scale later.

### D. Differentiation Messages

AI:

> WIZFIELD AI surfaces workspace-scoped signals, prepares drafts for review, supports guarded customer follow-up, and includes Voice Intake Intelligence as a bounded pilot.

Growth Center:

> Growth Center turns operational signals into marketing opportunities, drafts, campaigns, explicit publish jobs, and internal analytics.

Language Store:

> Language Store helps multilingual teams work with preferred language support while keeping supported customer-facing estimate and invoice output in reviewed English.

Multi-Business Control:

> Multi-Business Control lets one user identity operate multiple separated business workspaces through explicit workspace switching and clear active context.

### E. Conversion Message

Primary conversion message:

> Start a trial and see how WIZFIELD brings your field-service operations into one system.

Supporting conversion lines:

- Start with the operating system your business needs today.
- Bring the daily work into one connected place.
- Move from fragmented tools to operational clarity.
- Ready to evaluate WIZFIELD for your business? Start with a trial.

### F. Proof Language

Safe proof phrases:

- Built from real field operations.
- Designed for modern service businesses.
- Documented like a real platform.
- Grounded in workspace-scoped records.
- Human review stays in control.
- Publishing remains explicit.
- Customer-facing output is reviewed before it becomes final.
- Workspace context stays separated.
- Starter supports 1 business. Pro supports up to 3 businesses. Business is sales-assisted.

Avoid fake proof:

- No invented testimonials.
- No invented metrics.
- No guaranteed ROI.
- No implied customer count.
- No unsupported lead-generation claims.
