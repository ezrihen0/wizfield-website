# WIZFIELD Full Page Architecture Pack v1

## 1. Executive Summary

This document defines the canonical full page architecture for the remaining core WIZFIELD marketing pages at `www.wizfield.com`:

- `/platform`
- `/ai`
- `/growth-center`
- `/language-store`
- `/multi-business`
- `/pricing`
- `/features`
- `/contact`

It inherits the locked strategy from `WIZFIELD_Marketing_Website_Master_Plan_v1.md` and the locked homepage blueprint from `WIZFIELD_Homepage_Detailed_Wireframe_and_Copy_Map_v1.md`.

The architecture keeps these decisions fixed:

- WIZFIELD is positioned as the intelligent operating system for field-service businesses.
- Primary CTA is `Start Trial`.
- Secondary education CTA is `See How It Works`.
- Business/high-complexity path uses `Contact Sales`.
- Docs remains a product-confidence signal.
- Starter and Pro are visible; Business is `Contact Sales`.
- Voice AI is bounded as `Voice Intake Intelligence — Pilot`.
- Homepage structure is not reopened.

This pack is source material for Task 4 Marketing Copy System, Task 5 Website Visual Design System, and Task 6 Marketing Visual Asset Package. It is not final polished copy for every section and it does not authorize website implementation yet.

## 2. Cross-Site Page Architecture Strategy

The site should work as a layered evaluation system rather than a set of disconnected brochure pages.

Core narrative:

- Homepage sells the total thesis and conversion path.
- Platform page proves WIZFIELD is an operating system, not another CRM.
- AI page explains bounded intelligence safely and specifically.
- Growth Center page proves growth workflows are operationally connected and human-controlled.
- Language Store page explains a distinctive multilingual team layer with narrow customer-output truth.
- Multi-Business page proves structural scalability through explicit workspace switching and separated context.
- Pricing page converts high-intent buyers and routes Business buyers to sales.
- Features page organizes product breadth for comparison, SEO, and evaluator confidence.
- Contact page handles Business, complex operators, partnerships, and serious inquiries without becoming the default self-serve path.

Page depth logic:

- Product thesis pages (`/platform`, `/features`) should be broad and structural.
- Differentiator pages (`/ai`, `/growth-center`, `/language-store`, `/multi-business`) should go deep on product-specific truth boundaries.
- Conversion pages (`/pricing`, `/contact`) should reduce decision friction and clarify next action.

Visual logic:

- All pages use Liquid Platinum as the default canvas.
- Digital Indigo carries hierarchy and authority.
- Electric Cyan and Hyper Violet appear as intelligent edge energy, not decoration overload.
- Glass intelligence surfaces are reserved for hero visuals, product mockups, AI/Growth/Language highlights, system maps, and premium CTA panels.
- Product-heavy pages must show credible WIZFIELD UI mockups rather than vague abstract art.
- Richer energy is appropriate on `/ai`, `/growth-center`, and `/language-store`, but legibility and restraint remain mandatory.

Truth logic:

- AI surfaces signals, drafts, guarded send, outcome observation, and bounded voice intake pilot. It does not operate the business autonomously.
- Growth Center supports marketing profile, drafts, variants, CRM-derived opportunities, campaigns, explicit publish jobs, V1 automations, and internal analytics. It does not auto-publish or show ROI/ad dashboards.
- Language Store supports worker language enablement, per-user/per-org preference, reviewed English customer-output generation for specific estimate/invoice line flows, and snapshot-safe history. It does not translate everything.
- Multi-business supports one identity, multiple organizations/workspaces, explicit switching, full context change, data separation, and shared billing. It does not blend tenant data or claim cross-business intelligence dashboards.

## 3. Page-by-Page Architecture

### `/platform`

#### Strategic Site Role

Page mission:

- Explain why WIZFIELD is an operating system, not a basic CRM or disconnected stack.
- Make an evaluating visitor believe WIZFIELD has a coherent operating model across intake, customers, jobs, estimates, invoices, communication, reports, customer access, growth workflows, intelligence layers, and workspace control.

Primary audience state:

- Evaluating.
- Understands the homepage thesis and wants to know what is actually connected.
- May be comparing WIZFIELD to CRM, field-service management tools, or internal spreadsheet/tool stacks.

Primary CTA:

- `Start Trial`

Secondary CTA:

- `See How It Works` or `View Features`

Relationship to homepage:

- Expands the homepage Platform Thesis and Product Preview.
- Should not mechanically repeat the homepage hero or homepage section sequence.
- Should prove the operating-system claim in more detail through structure, system maps, module groups, workflow continuity, and disciplined product visuals.

#### Recommended Page Title / Strategic Headline Direction

> A field-service operating system, not another disconnected tool.

This is strategic headline direction, not final polished copy.

#### Recommended Section Order

1. Hero — Operating System Thesis
2. Platform Map — One Connected Operating Core
3. Core Operating Areas
4. Workflow Continuity
5. Product Discipline / Real Software Proof
6. Multi-Business Awareness
7. Intelligence Layers
8. Final CTA

#### Section-by-Section Architecture

Hero — Operating System Thesis:

- Section role: Re-state category and differentiate from CRM/point tools.
- Core message: WIZFIELD connects the daily work of field-service businesses into one operating environment.
- Content block type: Hero copy, primary CTA, secondary CTA, compact module proof row.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Platform-oriented hero mockup with WIZFIELD core, operational cards, and restrained connected energy.
- CTA: `Start Trial`; secondary `View Features` or `See How It Works`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: Avoid vague `all-in-one` filler unless immediately backed by real module categories.

Platform Map — One Connected Operating Core:

- Section role: Make the operating-system claim visually legible.
- Core message: Intake, customers, jobs, estimates, invoices, communication, reports, portal/customer access, Growth Center, and workspace control connect around one WIZFIELD core.
- Content block type: System map with labeled module cards.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Central WIZFIELD glass hub with calm cyan-to-violet routing lines.
- CTA: None required.
- Claims/truth guardrail: Do not imply every module has autonomous AI control.

Core Operating Areas:

- Section role: Show breadth without dumping features.
- Core message: WIZFIELD covers operational categories that field-service owners need daily.
- Content block type: Card grid grouped by area.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Product cards or small UI vignettes for intake, jobs, estimates/invoices, communication, reports, portal.
- CTA: `View Features`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: Do not claim vertical-specific workflows for every trade.

Workflow Continuity:

- Section role: Prove continuity from first call to follow-up.
- Core message: Context should carry forward instead of being rebuilt at every step.
- Content block type: Journey rail.
- Visual/mockup requirement: `Call / Lead -> Customer -> Job -> Estimate -> Approval -> Invoice -> Follow-up -> Growth Opportunity`.
- CTA: None required.
- Claims/truth guardrail: If AI follow-up appears, frame it as draft/review.

Product Discipline / Real Software Proof:

- Section role: Build confidence that the platform is real and usable.
- Core message: WIZFIELD product UI is operational first, with controlled intelligent energy.
- Content block type: Large dashboard/product shell preview with callouts.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Product dashboard with readable labels and realistic navigation.
- CTA: `Start Trial`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: No fake metrics, no unsupported dashboards.

Multi-Business Awareness:

- Section role: Introduce structural scale inside platform story.
- Core message: One user identity may operate multiple businesses/workspaces, with explicit switching and separated context.
- Content block type: Workspace switcher explanatory block.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Workspace switcher changing active dashboard context.
- CTA: `Explore Multi-Business`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: No blended cross-business analytics.

Intelligence Layers:

- Section role: Show AI, Growth Center, and Language Store as layers around the operating system.
- Core message: Intelligence and growth features sit on top of real operational records and controlled workflows.
- Content block type: Three layer cards.
- Visual/mockup requirement: AI card, Growth Center flow card, Language Store split-output card.
- CTA: Links to `/ai`, `/growth-center`, `/language-store`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: Each card must include bounded truth language.

Final CTA:

- Section role: Convert after platform proof.
- Core message: Bring field-service operations into one system.
- Content block type: Centered glass CTA panel.
- Visual/mockup requirement: Subtle W intelligence graph motif.
- CTA: `Start Trial`; secondary `View Pricing`.
- Claims/truth guardrail: Keep promise operational, not outcome-guaranteed.

#### Required Visual Asset Inventory

- Platform hero operating-system visual.
- Central platform map.
- Core operating area card set.
- Operational journey rail.
- Product dashboard preview.
- Multi-business workspace switcher preview.
- Intelligence layers card trio.
- Final CTA W intelligence motif.

#### Responsive Notes

- Desktop: Use wide system maps and horizontal journey rails. Product UI mockups should be large enough to read labels.
- Tablet: Convert system maps into balanced two-column grids. Journey rail may become two rows.
- Mobile: Stack sections with WIZFIELD core first, then modules. Convert journey to vertical timeline. Use cropped or mobile-adapted product previews instead of unreadable desktop screenshots.

#### Claims-Safety Notes

Potential drift:

- Generic `all-in-one` claims with no product proof.
- Vertical-specific workflow claims for trades not explicitly supported.
- Autonomous AI implications inside platform diagrams.
- Multi-business blended analytics.

Safe framing:

- `Operating system` means connected operating workflows and workspace context, not autonomous business operation.
- Multi-business means explicit workspace switching and separated context.

#### Build Priority

Launch-critical.

Reason: `/platform` is the core explanation page behind the homepage thesis and should exist before serious launch traffic.
